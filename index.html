Deep Red Hibiscus Rose

Deep Red
  • Dried hibiscus flowers
  • Rose petals (fairtrade)
  • Pomegranate extract
  • A whisper of cardamom
  • Pure spring water

Tasting note: A deep, vivid tartness softened by the romance of rose. Cooling on the palate, lingering florally. Best served over ice with a sprig of fresh mint.

Origin & craft: Hibiscus — karkadé in Egyptian — has been steeped for centuries across West Africa and the Middle East. We source ours from small fairtrade cooperatives in Burkina Faso, where petals are hand-harvested at peak bloom.

Rooibos Citrus

Amber–Red
  • South African rooibos (fairtrade)
  • Orange peel (organic)
  • Lemon verbena
  • Ginger root
  • Wildflower honey

Tasting note: Warm, woody, and naturally sweet — rooibos wraps you in comfort while zesty citrus cuts through with brightness. A grounding yet uplifting cup.

Origin & craft: Rooibos (Aspalathus linearis) grows only in the Cederberg mountains of South Africa — a plant of extraordinary resilience. Our rooibos is Rainforest Alliance certified and sourced through long-term cooperative partnerships that support local farming families.

Black Tea Peach

Warm Amber
  • First-flush Darjeeling black tea
  • White peach purée (organic)
  • Dried apricot
  • Vanilla pod
  • A touch of cane sugar

Tasting note: The muscatel depth of Darjeeling meets ripe summer peach in a long, honeyed finish. Silky, nostalgic, and beautifully balanced between tannic structure and fruit sweetness.

Origin & craft: Darjeeling's "first flush" — plucked in spring before the monsoon — is considered among the world's finest teas. We work exclusively with estates enrolled in the Darjeeling Fairtrade certification programme, ensuring fair wages for the workers who make these leaves possible.

Green Tea Lemongrass

Pale Gold
  • Japanese sencha green tea
  • Fresh lemongrass stalks
  • Kaffir lime leaf
  • Cucumber extract
  • Agave nectar (raw, organic)

Tasting note: Crisp, verdant, and citrus-luminous. The grassy freshness of sencha opens into aromatic lemongrass, with a cooling cucumber finish that is the very definition of refreshment.

Origin & craft: Sencha is Japan's most beloved everyday tea — steeped in a culture where preparation is itself a form of meditation. Our sencha is shade-grown by a third-generation family farm in Shizuoka, harvested by hand in early spring, then immediately steam-fixed to preserve every molecule of green vitality.

Tea Culture

Thousands of years
in every pour

Tea is not a commodity. It is an art, a ritual, a conversation between land and person. Nænsomt exists to honour that.

  1. 1. Garden & Harvest

    Every tea begins in soil cared for across generations. We partner only with gardens that practice regenerative agriculture — no synthetic pesticides, no monoculture.

  2. 2. Sun-Drying & Processing

    Leaves are dried by sun and low-temperature air — never by industrial heat. This preserves the volatile aromatics that define a tea's character and origin.

  3. 3. Cold-Brew Infusion

    We cold-brew for 12–18 hours in mountain spring water. No heat means no bitterness, no degraded antioxidants — only the pure, crystalline character of the leaf.

  4. 4. Bottled with Intention

    Nænsomt bottles in recycled glass, sealed with natural cork composite. Each label is printed on FSC-certified paper with soy-based inks. Nothing is an afterthought.

Ecology & Fairtrade

Good for the leaf.
Good for the world.

Premium means nothing if the soil is depleted and the farmers unpaid. We disagree with that version of premium.

Fairtrade Certified

Every ingredient carries Fairtrade certification. Farmers receive fair prices, safe conditions, and community development funds — not as charity, but as justice.

Recyclable Packaging

100% recycled glass bottles. Labels printed on FSC-certified paper. Cork-composite caps. Our packaging is designed for a second life — every time.

Low-Impact Production

Cold-brew processing uses 60% less energy than hot-fill methods. Our production site runs on 100% renewable electricity and harvests rainwater for cooling.

Transparent Supply Chain

We publish annual sourcing reports naming every farm and partner. Every bottle includes a batch code linking to its complete journey — from garden to glass.
